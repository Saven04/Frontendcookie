Cookie Policy

This Cookie Policy explains how NewsHub ("we," "us," or "our") uses cookies and similar technologies on our website, [yourwebsite.com]. It also outlines your rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and how you can manage your cookie preferences.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit our website. They help improve functionality, enhance user experience, and provide us with usage insights.

How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for these purposes:

  • Strictly Necessary Cookies: Essential for site functionality (e.g., authentication, session management). These cannot be disabled.
  • Performance Cookies: Collect anonymous data to analyze site performance and improve user experience.
  • Functional Cookies: Remember your preferences (e.g., theme, font size) for a personalized experience.
  • Advertising Cookies: Deliver relevant ads based on your interests and measure ad effectiveness.
  • Social Media Cookies: Enable sharing content on platforms like Facebook and Twitter.

Your Rights Under GDPR

Under GDPR, you have these rights regarding cookies and personal data:

  • Right to Access: Request details about your data and cookie usage.
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate data.
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion—non-essential cookies are reset immediately, with records retained for 2 years.
  • Right to Restrict Processing: Limit data processing.
  • Right to Object: Oppose processing (e.g., for advertising).
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a readable format.

Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You can manage cookies by:

  • Using the "Settings" section on our site to enable/disable non-essential cookies (strictly necessary cookies remain active).
  • Adjusting browser settings to block or delete cookies, though this may impact functionality.
  • Requesting deletion via "Delete Cookie Data," which resets non-essential preferences instantly.

Preferences are stored for 2 years unless deleted or updated. Deletion requests disable non-essential cookies, preserving your consent ID for future settings.
